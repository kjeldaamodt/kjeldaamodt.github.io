I am an Assistant Professor in Residence in the Division of Craniofacial Anomalies, Department of Orofacial Sciences at UCSF. My interests lie in basic research, specifically in the molecular and cellular mechanisms of tooth development, renewal, and movement. In my laboratory, we utilize genetically modified mouse models to gain further understanding of the functional roles of genes and signaling pathways. The ultimate goal is to innovate or improve upon current preventive, diagnostic, and therapeutic methods in dentistry by translating biological data from basic research for application into the clinic. Concomitant to my research, I am a part-time Orthodontic resident and plan to be certified by the American Board of Orthodontics in 2017. 

Dr. Andrew Jheon
